\noindent Dear Nominal Systems Team,

Nominal is working on a problem I’ve returned to throughout my career: helping autonomy teams test, debug, and deploy real systems safely when hardware, data, and operators are all in the loop.

During my PhD at Georgia Tech, I studied safe autonomy and trajectory optimization for autonomous spacecraft. I collaborated on robotics and aerospace research with NASA JPL, MIT Lincoln Lab, and AFRL. I was part of the founding team of the Robotarium, where I built safety verification tooling that allowed thousands of users to run code on physical robots without damaging hardware.

Since then, I’ve spent four years as a technical founder. At Pytheia, I built and deployed a real-time multi-camera 3D perception system. At Roostr, I built long-running agentic systems for freight operations that handled noisy, real-world data and ran reliably in production. Across both, the work was about connecting algorithms to hardware and building systems that hold up outside the lab.

The Roboticist-in-Residence role aligns closely with how I like to work: hands-on with hardware, debugging control and communication issues, building tooling, and deploying alongside customers. I’d be excited to help Nominal’s customers ship autonomy faster and with confidence.
