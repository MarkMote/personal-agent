# Behavioral Questions

Answer format: Situation → Task → Action → Result → Long-term effect

---

**Tell me about a time you delivered a high-stakes project with zero documentation or guidance.**
- Tip: Scale loves this. FDEs work in SCIFs with no internet.
- Answer: Early days of building agents at pytheia, really no rulebooks in those days and we had some big contracts on the line

**Tell me about a time you found a "good enough" solution that was a bottleneck, and tore it down to build something 10x faster.**
- Answer: Thats been the entire premise of Roostr. We didn't start as a freight forwarder, we started by selling business analytics to freight forwarders...

**Tell me about a time you convinced a skeptical, high-value stakeholder your technical approach was correct.**
- Tip: FDEs tell Generals and CEOs "no, the model can't do that yet."
- Answer: Traffic camera thing at pytheia...

**Tell me about a time you noticed a subtle but critical flaw that everyone else overlooked.**
- Tip: Anthropic cares about this—alignment and safety.
- Answer: In my phd, I studied optimal trajectory planning. I noticed that there was really no protocol for what to do when the vehicle was about to crash - you studied how not to crash into something, but with absolutely no playbook on what to do if the collision was inevitable. I developed the first trajectory optimization schema that modeled collisions, and let you minimize damage. 

**Tell me about a time you chose a harder technical path because it was the right way, even though it delayed launch.**
- Answer: Roostr we could have just hired a large number of people in the phillipines like every other freight forwarder and had a nice business

**Tell me about a time you explained highly complex AI/technical behavior to someone with zero technical background.**
- Answer: I helped to pay my way through undergrad by working as a tutor, but I'd also say when I did sales at pytheia, we sold real-time multi-camera computer vision to a donut shop. 

**Tell me about a time you debugged a problem where you couldn't tell if the issue was software, hardware, or environment.**
- Tip: Viam loves this. Sensors fail, WiFi drops, motors stall.
- Answer: In undergrad we built a zero-gravity enabling quadcopter 

**Tell me about a project where you optimized for extreme resource constraints (memory, CPU, latency).**
- Answer: We were originally designing pytheia, for autonomous vehicle perception so latency was everything. Had to put certain modules in C++. 

**Tell me about a time you built a tool because the existing tools were garbage.**
- Answer: Early days of llms, i built a lot of my own coding tools. The most used exaple today is a language learning app. 

**Tell me about a time you were wrong about a technical direction and how you pivoted.**
- Tip: Shows coachability. Don't say "I wasn't really wrong."
- Answer: I underestimated the difficulty of automating a quoting workflow in Roostr. I was confident i could get it done in so much time, so we negotiated and timeboxed a one week experiement and i failed. 

**Why do you want to be an engineer instead of starting a 3rd company?**
- Answer: I think its all about scale and the ability to immeditely work with elite teams 

**How do you handle not being in charge?**
- Answer: I've learned no ones in charge, you just answer to different people. Even if you're the CEO the customer is your boss. But I also really just care about being effective and getting the work done. Egos not important, credits not important, the only thing that matters is results. 

**Tell me about a time you shipped something under extreme time pressure.**
- Answer: One month we couldnt pay rent at pytheia

**Tell me about a project that failed. What happened and what did you learn?**
- Answer: Pytheias first idea. Learned there is a lot more that goes into a business than the abliity to solve a technical problem. 

**Tell me about a time you had to make a decision with incomplete information.**
- Answer: Took a big risk pivoting roostr last year. The saas was going well. We didnt really know how well it would go. 

**Walk me through the hardest bug you've ever debugged.**
- Answer: Quadcopter integrator windup. 

**Tell me about a time you had to learn a new technology quickly to solve a problem.**
- Answer: LLMs help a lot. Trust your intution, be persistent. Try to reinvent it. 

**Tell me about a time you disagreed with a colleague on a technical approach. How did you resolve it?**
- Answer: Quoting bot. 

**Tell me about a time you took ownership of something outside your job description.**
- Answer: Marketing at roostr - its a CEO thing but i was the better one for it. 
- I think most things people do wrong in business and engineering is some form of "confusing the means with the ends" -  job descriptions are a means to guide you to helping the business thrive, they are not the end in itself. Your real job description is just to make the company win at the thing its trying to win at. 

**Tell me about a time you identified a problem no one asked you to solve.**
- Answer: collisions in robots.
