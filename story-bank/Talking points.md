

What is "the hard part" in engineering
Here's what i think people miss: coding was never the hard part. 
The hard part was figuring out what to build and understanding users well enough to actually know what they need. 
Its getting stakeholders on board, or making a decision with incomplete information. Its not knowledge, its taste, its judgement. 




> “One thing startups taught me is how dangerous unvalidated intuition can be. I’m very opinionated about system design, but I care much more about being right than being original. I’m happy to run experiments, defer to domain experts, and update quickly when evidence changes.”

This signals:
- Strong opinions.
- Low ego attachment.
- Willingness to integrate.

> “In startups you optimize for speed and survival. In larger systems, you optimize for reliability, maintainability, and coordination. I actually enjoy learning how good teams scale those tradeoffs.”

This tells them:
- You understand different operating regimes.
- You won’t blindly import startup behavior into the wrong environment.


> Right now I’m specifically looking for a place where I can go deep for several years on a hard technical problem with a strong team.”

That signals stability without defensiveness.
