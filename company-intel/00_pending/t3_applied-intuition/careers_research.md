## Relevant Roles Found

### 1. Research Scientist - Simulation
- **Location:** Sunnyvale, CA only
- **Salary:** $158,000 - $304,000 USD
- **Requirements:**
  - MSc or PhD in machine learning, computer vision/graphics with autonomy and robotics applications
  - Strong publication record in top-tier conferences/journals
  - Passion for scalable simulation and world models for real-world autonomy
- **Research Areas:** World foundation models, 3D/4D reconstruction, novel view synthesis, traffic simulation, deformable object simulation
- **Fit:** Strong match for PhD background. Heavy ML/CV focus may not leverage controls/optimization expertise as well.

### 2. Research Scientist - Robotics
- **Location:** Sunnyvale, CA only
- **Salary:** $158,000 - $304,000 USD
- **Requirements:**
  - MSc or PhD in ML/CV with robotics/autonomy focus
  - Strong publication record
  - Passion for "robotic generalist and humanoid"
- **Research Areas:** Robotic foundation models, vision-language-action models, RL, dexterous manipulation, whole-body control
- **Fit:** Good match if pivoting toward humanoid robotics. Less aligned with spacecraft/controls background.

### 3. Research Scientist - Autonomy
- **Location:** Sunnyvale, CA only
- **Salary:** $158,000 - $304,000 USD (estimated, same band)
- **Requirements:**
  - MSc or PhD in ML/CV with autonomy/robotics applications
  - Strong research skills
- **Research Areas:** End-to-end autonomy, vision-language-action models, reinforcement learning
- **Fit:** Decent alignment with autonomy background, but heavily ML-focused.

### 4. Software Engineer - Controls
- **Location:** Mountain View, CA only
- **Salary:** $125,000 - $222,000 USD
- **Requirements:**
  - MSc or PhD in mechanical engineering or CS with coursework/research in controls
  - Experience with safety-critical C++ (MISRA, AUTOSAR)
  - Experience with QP solvers, nonlinear optimization (OSQP, SCS, CasADi)
  - Experience applying ML to vehicle dynamics modeling
  - Experience with large industrial vehicles in off-road environments
- **Focus:** Controls modules for off-road autonomous vehicles, nonlinear MPC, MPPI control
- **Fit:** EXCELLENT match for controls/optimization PhD. This is the bullseye role.

### 5. Decision Autonomy Engineer (EpiSci subsidiary)
- **Location:** Washington DC, Mountain View, San Diego, Ann Arbor, Fort Walton Beach
- **Salary:** $155,000 - $190,000 USD
- **Requirements:**
  - MS/PhD in CS, Robotics, Optimization, or related field OR 3+ years developing intelligent agent systems
  - Strong algorithmic complexity, graph theory, optimization
  - Experience with constraint satisfaction, C++/Python
  - Preferred: PhD in algorithms/optimization, publications in multi-agent coordination, PDDL/HTN planning, game theory
- **Security:** Requires US citizenship and SECRET clearance eligibility
- **Fit:** Strong match for optimization background. Defense focus may be less appealing than commercial autonomy.

### 6. Autonomy Engineer (various specializations)
- **Locations:** Fort Walton Beach, Washington DC, Irvine, San Diego, Ann Arbor
- **Salary:** $120,000 - $180,000 USD
- **Requirements:**
  - Bachelor's in CS or engineering
  - 2+ years developing software for autonomous robotic systems
  - Python or C++ proficiency
  - US citizenship, clearance eligibility
- **Specializations:** Air Combat Autonomy, Multi-Agent Teaming, Aerial Autonomy
- **Fit:** Defense-focused. Lower seniority and compensation than Research Scientist roles.

### 7. Systems Engineering, Controls - Autonomy Trucking
- **Location:** Sunnyvale, CA
- **Focus:** Trucking autonomy controls
- **Fit:** Good alignment but trucking-specific.

## Best Fit Role

**Primary Target: Software Engineer - Controls**
- Direct alignment with PhD in optimization and controls
- Nonlinear MPC, MPPI, optimal control - core competencies
- Experience with QP solvers/CasADi maps to optimization background
- Vehicle dynamics modeling connects to spacecraft GNC experience

**Secondary Target: Research Scientist - Simulation**
- Higher comp band ($158k-$304k vs $125k-$222k)
- Publications and PhD carry weight
- World models and simulation align with academic research style
- Weakness: Heavy ML/CV emphasis vs traditional controls

**Alternative: Decision Autonomy Engineer**
- Optimization and multi-agent coordination align well
- Multiple location options
- Downside: Defense focus, clearance requirement, lower comp

## Application Notes

### Work Environment
- **5 days/week in-office** is the expectation
- "Occasional remote work" and flexible hours acknowledged
- Global rotation program available after first year

### Interview Process
- Not detailed on job postings
- Likely standard: recruiter screen, technical phone screens, onsite

### Compensation Structure
- Base salary + equity (options and/or RSUs)
- 100% employee premiums paid for health/dental/vision
- 50% 401(k) match
- Learning stipends, wellness benefits

### Application Strategy
1. **Location decision required:** All relevant roles are CA-based. Need to decide if willing to relocate or if this is a dealbreaker.
2. **Dual-track application:** Could apply to both Software Engineer - Controls and Research Scientist - Simulation to see which tracks better.
3. **Narrative:** Position as controls/optimization expert from spacecraft domain transitioning to autonomous vehicles. Publications + industry experience combo is differentiator.

### Red Flags / Concerns
- No NYC presence for engineering = geographic mismatch with Wave 1 strategy
- 5 days/week in-office is strict
- Research roles heavily ML-focused, may undervalue classical controls expertise
