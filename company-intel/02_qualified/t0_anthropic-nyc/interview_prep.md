## Your Core Narrative

**The Safety → AI Journey:**
My career has been driven by a single thread: building safe autonomous systems. I believed autonomous vehicles would be the defining safety challenge of my generation, so I focused my PhD on spacecraft control safety and started a computer vision company to make self-driving cars safer.

When LLMs achieved breakthrough capability, we pivoted—not chasing a trend, but recognizing that a fundamental threshold had been crossed. The most transformative technology in human history wasn't decades away; it was happening now, in my lifetime.

We successfully built and scaled an LLM SaaS product, learned from customers, and proved the business model. I then joined Roostr because it represented the most ambitious LLM application I could find: using AI to directly operate a business and consolidate the fragmented trillion-dollar freight industry.

Roostr has been successful—we've grown revenue and built real AI systems that handle complex logistics. But as I've worked in production AI systems, I've realized my deeper calling is to help shape the foundational technology itself, not just apply it.

**Why Anthropic FDE:**
I understand this isn't a safety research role, but it's an opportunity to contribute to the mission during the most critical period in AI development. The FDE role combines my product engineering experience with direct impact on how transformative AI gets deployed responsibly. It's a chance to work on the most important technology of our time while it's still being shaped.
