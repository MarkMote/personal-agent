# Icarus Robotics

## Quick Facts
- **Location:** Brooklyn, NY
- **Stage:** Seed ($6.1M, Sep 2025) - Soma Capital, Xtal, Nebular, Massive Tech
- **Team Size:** ~10 (earliest technical hires)
- **Focus:** Dexterous robots for space station logistics

## The Problem They're Solving
Astronauts spend ~75% of time on cargo logistics ("Amazon warehouse workers with PhDs"). Every 60 days, 3.5 tons of cargo arrives at ISS - all must be unpacked and stowed manually. $130k/hr astronaut labor bottleneck.

## Their Approach
- Fan-propelled robot with two arms + jaw grippers (not humanoid)
- Bimanual manipulation gets 80% of needed dexterity with simple grippers
- Start teleoperated (labor arbitrage margin is huge), build to autonomy via embodied AI
- Collect microgravity data with human-in-loop → foundational models for orbital robotics

## Tech Stack
- ROS2 on embedded (Jetson, RPi)
- Isaac Sim, Gazebo, MATLAB/Simulink for simulation
- Focus: underactuated systems, safety-critical control, fault-tolerant design

## Milestones
- Completed terrestrial long-distance teleop demo (bimanual cargo bag operations)
- Parabolic flight campaign coming up (early 2026)
- 1-year ISS demo via Voyager Space (Bishop airlock) - "JOYRIDE-1"
- Path: cargo ops → station maintenance (filter/seal inspections) → full autonomy for deep space
