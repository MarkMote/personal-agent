# Full Context: Laurion Capital

_Unstructured reference document for all company research, notes, and context._

## Opportunity Assessment

### Why This Is Interesting
- **$6B AUM** - substantial fund size, well-established
- **4-person engineering team** - extremely high leverage per engineer  
- **Agentic systems focus** - cutting-edge intersection of AI and finance
- **Front Office role** - direct impact on trading P&L, not back-office support
- **Manhattan location** - top-tier financial district presence

### Strategic Fit Analysis
**Strengths for Mark:**
- ✅ **Agentic systems experience** - Roostr's AI agents managing freight operations
- ✅ **Production LLM systems** - proven ability to deploy AI in business-critical applications
- ✅ **Founder background** - understand pressure of systems handling real money
- ✅ **Technical depth** - PhD-level problem solving for complex systems
- ✅ **Startup to enterprise** - experience scaling systems under pressure

**Potential Concerns:**
- ❓ **Finance domain knowledge** - limited direct trading/quant experience
- ❓ **Small team dynamics** - very different from startup founder role
- ❓ **Hedge fund culture** - intense, high-pressure environment
- ❓ **5 days onsite** - significant lifestyle change from current flexibility

### Compensation Context
- **Industry benchmark:** Top quant funds pay $300-600k+ for senior engineers
- **Small team premium:** 4 engineers supporting $6B suggests high individual impact/compensation
- **Front Office bonus:** Likely significant performance-based compensation tied to fund performance
- **Comparison:** Should be competitive with Jane Street, potentially higher due to hedge fund structure

## Technical Context

### Agentic Systems in Finance
- **Trading agents:** Automated execution, risk management, portfolio optimization
- **Research agents:** Data analysis, pattern recognition, alpha generation
- **Operations agents:** Trade settlement, compliance monitoring, reporting
- **Risk agents:** Real-time risk assessment, position sizing, drawdown management

### Critical Requirements
- **Latency:** Microsecond execution requirements for some strategies
- **Reliability:** System failures directly impact P&L - no room for errors
- **Risk management:** Must handle position limits, stop-losses, regulatory constraints
- **Integration:** Work with existing trading infrastructure, market data feeds
- **Monitoring:** Real-time alerting, performance tracking, audit trails

## Market Context

### Quantitative Trading Landscape
- **Tier 1:** Renaissance, DE Shaw, Citadel Securities, Two Sigma
- **Tier 2:** PDT, Hudson River Trading, Jane Street (market making)
- **Tier 3:** Smaller focused funds like Laurion

### Competitive Analysis
**Vs. Jane Street:**
- Jane Street = Market making, more structured interview process
- Laurion = Directional trading(?), smaller team, potentially more autonomy

**Vs. Big Tech:**
- Hedge funds typically pay more total comp
- Less long-term equity, more immediate cash compensation
- Faster feedback loops (daily P&L vs quarterly releases)

## Interview Strategy

### Key Messages
1. **AI in Production:** "I've built AI systems that handle real business operations daily"
2. **Risk awareness:** "Coming from a founder background, I understand systems that can lose money"
3. **Scale efficiency:** "Experience building systems that scale with small, high-leverage teams"
4. **Learning orientation:** "Eager to apply AI expertise in the sophisticated environment of quantitative finance"

### Questions That Differentiate You
- "How do you handle the reliability requirements when AI agents are making trading decisions?"
- "What's the feedback loop between the models' performance and the engineering team?"
- "How do you balance innovation with the risk management constraints of a trading environment?"
- "What's the collaboration like between the engineering team and the portfolio managers?"

### What NOT to Ask
- Basic compensation questions (save for later rounds)
- Work-life balance specifics (hedge fund context)
- Generic "what's the culture like" questions
- Anything that reveals lack of finance domain knowledge
