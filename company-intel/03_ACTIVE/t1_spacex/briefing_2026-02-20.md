- **V1.5 satellites:** Legacy fleet, being phased out
- **V2 Mini:** Current workhorse (2,000+ in orbit). ~60 Gbps each, 4x V1 capacity. Argon-fueled Hall thrusters (replacing krypton), 2.4x thrust and 1.5x Isp over V1. Up to 28 per Falcon 9 launch.

## Three Big GNC Challenges Right Now (Talking Point Gold)

### 1. Orbit Lowering Campaign (Jan 2026)
- Lowering **4,400 satellites from ~550 km to ~480 km** throughout 2026
- Announced Jan 1 by Michael Nicolls (VP Starlink Engineering)
- Rationale: solar minimum → lower atmospheric density → slower natural decay at 550 km. At 480 km, ballistic decay time drops >80%
- Massive coordination challenge: thousands of individual maneuvers while maintaining service continuity
- Coordinated with US Space Command and other operators

### 2. Stargaze SSA System (Jan 30, 2026 — brand new)
- Space situational awareness built on Starlink's own star trackers (~30,000 across the fleet)
- Detects ~30 million object transits daily — orders of magnitude better than ground-based systems
- Can assess conjunctions in minutes (vs. hours for current standard)
- Real-world proof: detected a third-party satellite's unexpected maneuver with only 5 hrs to go, Starlink reacted within 1 hr
- Offered free to all satellite operators

### 3. V3 Vehicle Transition
- V3 satellites are 3x heavier (~2,000 kg) with completely different mass/drag properties
- New orbit regimes, new propulsion characteristics
- GNC team must develop and validate algorithms for a new vehicle class
- First launches imminent (H1 2026 via Starship)

### Bonus: Dec 2025 Anomaly
- Satellite Starlink-35956 suffered propulsion anomaly at 418 km, caused debris
- SpaceX deployed fleet-wide software fixes
- Partly motivated the orbit-lowering decision
- Shows GNC reliability and anomaly resilience are high-priority
