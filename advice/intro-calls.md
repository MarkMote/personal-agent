# Intro Call Mental Model (Short Version)

## Purpose

* Bidirectional filter, not an interview.
* Decide: **worth deeper time or not.**
* Optimize for **information density per minute.**
* Do not sell. Do not commit. Do not over-explain.

---

## Power Frame

* You are evaluating them as much as they are evaluating you.
* Neutral, precise, curious.
* Avoid supplicant posture.

---

## Call Flow

### 1. Opening (0–2 min)

**Goal:** establish context quickly.

If asked “tell me about yourself”:

* Current role / identity
* Core capability
* What you’re evaluating next

30–45 seconds max.

---

### 2. Core (5–20 min)

**Goal:** extract reality.

You should drive most of this with questions.

Focus on:

* What the role actually does day-to-day
* Decision authority and autonomy
* Incentives and equity
* Concrete examples of past outcomes
* How success is measured
* Who you’d work with

---

### 3. Close (2–5 min)

**Goal:** alignment + next step.

* Is this worth going deeper?
* What would the next conversation cover?

No commitment.

---

## High-Value Questions (Pick 4–6)

**Role reality**

* “How is time actually spent week to week?”
* “What have past people in this role shipped or owned?”

**Authority**

* “What decisions can I make independently?”
* “What requires approval?”

**Upside**

* “How does equity or carry work in practice?”
* “If something spins out, what would I own?”

**Track record**

* “Who else has done this role and where are they now?”
* “What’s the strongest outcome you’ve produced so far?”

**Scope clarity**

* “What does X mean concretely to you?”

**Time horizon**

* “Is this full-time and exclusive?”
* “Typical duration before outcome?”

---

## What to Listen For

**Good signals**

* Specific examples
* Numbers, names, timelines
* Clear ownership
* Willingness to say “we don’t know”
* Consistent answers

**Bad signals**

* Buzzwords
* Vague optimism
* Handwaving on equity or authority
* No concrete past examples

---

## Common Mistakes

* Over-talking or over-justifying
* Treating it like a test to pass
* Avoiding hard questions
* Getting emotionally anchored early

---

## Post-Call Debrief (2 minutes)

Write:

* What was concrete vs vague?
* Did incentives align?
* Did I respect their thinking?
* Is this worth deeper time?

Classify quickly:

* Strong pursue
* Maybe / gather more signal
* Deprioritize
