If you are applying with the same idea as a previous batch, did anything change? If you applied with a different idea, why did you pivot and what did you learn from the last idea? 
Previous ideas and what we learned:
We previously applied with the idea of offering perception solutions to autonomous vehicles (via traffic cameras) and robots. With autonomous vehicles, we learned that this would be a difficult place to begin as it would place high technical requirements on an MVP, involve selling to highly risk-averse entities with long acquisition cycles, and connecting many moving parts between government and AV companies. Robotics alleviated some but not all of these problems and it appears that the timing is too early to hit a large enough market with this alone. 

Why we pivoted: 
We began our pivot to management automation after speaking with a number of brick-and-mortar businesses. In addition to being a bigger market, we learned that robotics-grade perception could be used to overcome many of the limitations of traditional computer vision solutions in these areas. For example, most solutions only provide information in terms of the pixels of camera (the image frame). With this limitation you can’t solve problems that involve knowing where things are located in the real world, nor can you combine the information of multiple cameras into a single representation.  

Vision:
We would eventually like to build application-agnostic perception software to help any business use perception, but we have found that the easiest way to validate this idea is to build initially in the area with the greatest need.

Who are your competitors? What do you understand about your business that they don't? 
Verkada and SpotAI both provide cloud-based computer vision and analytics solutions. Verkada has a focus on security applications with a fully integrated system, while SpotAI offers stand-alone cloud services. SpotAI improves over Verkada by offering a wider set of capable analytics, but the offering is still rather limited because their analytics come from the 2-dimensional image frame. By instead working in the spatial frame we make it possible to define a broader set of analytics, including things like real-world walking/ line speeds. There are dedicated spatial analytics companies, such as VergeSense and Density, but they require specialized hardware, whereas our software interfaces with (any) existing CCTV camera. 
From our user interviews and design partner conversations, we’ve determined that ease-of-use is very important for adoption, particularly in restaurants. While Verkada and SpotAI have good platforms for custom configuration of analytics, we believe that users want an easier way to interact with the system. In addition to working in a (more intuitive) spatial frame, we’ve addressed this by building a chat feature that allows users to interact with the system in natural language. 


How do or will you make money? How much could you make? 
Examining just restaurants, specifically fast-casual like our pilot: 
There are 1M restaurants in the US, and of those 100k are fast-casual. Each has 2+ managers, costing $20B+ per year across the fast casual market. If we can increase manager productivity by 30%, we believe we can charge 10k / year / restaurant [which leaves value for the customer]. To make $ 100M yearly, we would need to be in 10k locations and we think this is achievable if we partner with food technology providers that have established distribution to these types of restaurants, such as POS makers.
Go-To-Market:
If we are successful in the pilot with our design partner, we should be able to scale our relationship with them to a dozen stores. At the same time, we are looking at partnering with other restaurant technology providers, such as point-of-sale services. Once we’ve proved the case, we will approach larger chains. In the long run (after establishing the restaurant vertical), we hope to move into other verticals such as retail, warehousing and medical.
