# Inbound Opportunity Policy

## Philosophy

Inbound is a **filtering and option-generation channel**, not a primary search driver. Outbound remains primary. Treat inbound with controlled openness—extract signal quickly, avoid commitment, don't let random recruiters steer strategy.

---

## Hard Gates (Check First)

Before scoring, kill anything that fails these:

1. **NYC-compatible?** Remote-friendly or NYC-based. No relocation required (unless truly exceptional).
2. **Not obviously misaligned?** No generic SWE staffing, offshore recruiters, low-signal SaaS, "hustle culture" vibes.

If it fails a hard gate → polite decline, no scoring needed.

---

## Scoring Framework (0-2 each, max 8)

### A. Skill Compounding
- 2 = Builds robotics / autonomy / controls / simulation depth
- 1 = Builds transferable systems, optimization, or quantitative skill
- 0 = Generic software, ops, or business work

### B. Wealth Upside
- 2 = Real chance at meaningful equity or high comp (top quant, breakout startup, big tech senior)
- 1 = Solid comp but capped
- 0 = Low comp, low equity, slow growth

### C. Option Value
- 2 = Improves credibility for future elite roles or founding (brand, network, skills)
- 1 = Neutral
- 0 = Narrows future options or pigeonholes you

### D. Execution Friction
- 2 = Easy conversation, low prep, fast signal
- 1 = Moderate cost
- 0 = High time sink, long loops, heavy prep

---

## Decision Rules

### Standard Mode (Phase 2)
- **Engage if score ≥ 6/8**
- **Or ≥ 5/8 with at least one dimension scoring 2**
- Everything else → polite decline or deprioritize

### Calibration Mode (Phase 1: First 2-3 weeks)
- **Lower threshold to ≥ 4/8** for opportunities that offer interview practice
- Prioritize companies you're NOT targeting for top offers—get reps without burning best shots
- Goal: calibrate narrative, identify weak spots, see what questions come up
- Cap at 2-3 practice conversations, then tighten back to standard mode

---

## Strategic Fit Check

Even if something scores well, ask: **Does this advance the barbell strategy?**

- **Stability/Wealth end:** Top quant, big tech senior, elite AI lab → YES
- **Mission/Equity end:** Robotics/autonomy startup with real depth, founding-track role → YES
- **Mushy middle:** Decent but generic, neither capital accelerator nor mission play → SKEPTICAL

Don't let a 5/8 opportunity in the mushy middle distract from pursuing 7/8 opportunities on the ends.

---

## Auto-Categorization

### ✅ Always engage (if passes hard gates)
- Top-tier quant (Jane Street, Citadel, Two Sigma, DE Shaw tier)
- Elite AI labs (DeepMind, OpenAI, Anthropic, FAIR)
- Robotics/autonomy companies with real technical depth
- Top-tier VC platforms for FIR (Lux, a]16z, Sequoia tier)

### ⚠️ Engage cautiously (score carefully)
- Generic startup recruiters
- Mid-tier VC FIRs
- Consulting / fractional CTO
- Companies outside core domains

### ❌ Auto-decline
- Generic SWE staffing firms
- Offshore recruiters
- Low-signal SaaS startups
- Crypto/web3 (unless exceptional and specific)
- Anything requiring relocation to SF

---

## Time Budget

- **Max 3-4 inbound conversations per week**
- **Max 30 minutes each** unless clearly high signal
- **Never let inbound consume > 20% of weekly job-search bandwidth**
- Outbound remains primary

---

## Response Templates

### Default Response
> Thanks for reaching out. I'm currently exploring senior technical roles in robotics / autonomy / AI systems, primarily NYC-based. If you think there's alignment, I'm happy to do a short intro call to understand the opportunity.

### For Quant Inbound
> I'm currently focused on robotics / AI roles, but I'm open to selectively exploring quantitative roles with strong technical depth and compensation. If this is in that category, happy to do a quick intro call.

### For FIR Inbound
> I've previously founded and led startups, so I'm selective about FIR programs. Can you share the fund, check size, partner involvement model, and typical path from FIR to company formation?

If they can't answer crisply → disengage.

### Polite Decline
> Thanks for thinking of me. This isn't quite aligned with what I'm focused on right now, but I appreciate you reaching out.

---

## Emotional Discipline

Do not:
- Get excited by early praise
- Anchor on hypothetical upside
- Mentally "accept" anything before full comparison set exists

Inbound often feels flattering. That's not signal.

**Real signal is:**
- Who else they've hired
- Actual comp / equity terms
- Real authority and funding
- Technical caliber of peers

Fast turnaround. Don't let inbound sit and create mental overhead.
