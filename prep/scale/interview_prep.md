Welcome to your upcoming technical interview! This interview will assess your ability to process structured data, perform transformations, and compute statistics. You will be implementing solutions to real-world problems that involve working with structured data formats and time intervals. This guide provides helpful information to prepare for the interview without revealing specific details about the problem statement.

**What to Expect**

The interview will be approximately 55 minutes long.
● This interview has two parts. You're expected to complete both. Factoring in introductions and time for questions, you'll have about 25 minutes per part.
● It will focus on data processing, time-based calculations, and structured problem-solving.
● You will be provided with a structured dataset and will be asked to compute relevant statistics.
● Expect to work with JSON data and perform basic data manipulations and interval calculations.
● The problem is more implementation-heavy than algorithmically complex.
● You will be given time at the end to refactor your code and discuss testing strategies.

**How to Prepare**
● Practice JSON Handling
  - Work with JSON parsing libraries in your preferred language.
  - Extract relevant fields from nested structures.
● Brush Up on Time Manipulation
  - Work with datetime libraries to parse and compute time ranges.
  - Practice merging overlapping time intervals.
● Solve Interval-Based Problems
  - Find problems related to merging time intervals.
  - Work on computing gaps between time periods.
● Write Modular & Clean Code
  - Avoid monolithic functions; break down logic into smaller reusable functions.
  - Focus on clear naming conventions and structured code.
● Think About Edge Cases
  - Consider inputs with missing values or extreme cases.
  - Be prepared to discuss potential pitfalls in your implementation.

**During the Interview**
● Ask clarifying questions if anything is unclear.
● Talk through your approach before writing code.
● Use helper functions to keep your code organized.
● Optimize where needed, but focus on correctness first.
● Be prepared to discuss testing and improvements at the end.
